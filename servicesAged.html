Aged care residential
facility and hospitals

Collaborate to support clients’ care needs

Aged care residential facilities and hospitals work closely together to provide comprehensive care for clients, ensuring that their medical, emotional, and social needs are met effectively. Care teams from both settings communicate and share important information to provide continuity of care for clients transitioning between aged care homes and hospitals, leading to improved overall well-being and outcomes by ensuring they receive coordinated and integrated care.

Provide nursing care for clients in aged care homes or recovering in hospitals

Skilled nurses in aged care residential facilities and hospitals assess, plan, implement, and evaluate care to ensure the health and safety of clients. They work collaboratively with other healthcare professionals to provide holistic care, coordinating treatments, medications, and therapies for each client's individual needs.

Experienced healthcare workers assist with daily living activities and medication management

Experienced healthcare workers in aged care residential facilities and hospitals provide essential assistance to clients with daily living activities, medication management, and overall well-being. They support clients with tasks like bathing, dressing, grooming, and eating, ensure proper medication administration, monitor for side effects, and communicate any concerns to nursing staff. This dedicated support promotes independence, dignity, and enhances the quality of life for each individual through personalized care and attention.

Staff are available for various shifts and leave coverage

Aged care residential facilities and hospitals operate 24/7 with staff working various shifts to provide round-the-clock care. This includes covering for colleagues on leave to ensure continuity of care and adequate staffing levels. The flexibility in scheduling and staffing enables consistent, high-quality care for clients at all times.
